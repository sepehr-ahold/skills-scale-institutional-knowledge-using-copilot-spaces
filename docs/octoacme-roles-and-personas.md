# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the deployment process and coordinate final release checklists. They ensure all pre-release criteria are met and serve as the central point of coordination between technical, product, and quality stakeholders at go-live.

### Responsibilities
- Oversee the end-to-end deployment process and maintain the release checklist
- Ensure all pre-release criteria are met, including documentation, sign-off, and stakeholder communication
- Collaborate with Technical Lead and Product Owner for go-live decisions
- Manage release scheduling and coordinate rollback plans if needed

### Goals
- Achieve smooth, low-risk deployments with clear accountability
- Ensure no release proceeds without full sign-off from relevant stakeholders
- Minimize post-release incidents through thorough pre-release validation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Product Owner** for feature sign-off and release readiness confirmation
- Collaborates with **Technical Lead** (or Development Team) for technical validation and deployment execution
- Coordinates with **QA Tester / Quality Lead** to confirm regression testing is complete before release

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reports and go/no-go decision summaries
- Pre-release checklists shared with all stakeholders
- Post-release deployment notifications and incident reports

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads define quality standards, testing protocols, and acceptance criteria across the project. They review deliverables, track defects, and ensure remediation plans are in place before any release proceeds.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards, testing protocols, and acceptance criteria
- Review deliverables at key milestones and track open defects
- Ensure remediation plans are created and addressed before release
- Work closely with Release Manager to confirm product readiness

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production through rigorous quality gates
- Establish repeatable testing processes that scale with project complexity
- Drive a culture of quality across the entire delivery team

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Collaborates with **QA Tester** to design and execute test plans and verify deliverable quality
- Works with **Release Manager** to confirm quality criteria are met before deployment
- Partners with **Product Owner** to define and refine acceptance criteria for each feature

### Typical Communication
- Quality reports and defect dashboards shared at sprint reviews
- Acceptance criteria documentation and test plans
- Remediation tracking updates communicated to the broader team

---

## Change Champion

### Role Summary
Change Champions support project acceptance and adoption by communicating changes across teams, identifying potential resistance, and facilitating training and enablement. They act as a bridge between project leadership and the broader organization during transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate project changes, impacts, and benefits across all affected teams
- Identify potential areas of resistance and gather feedback from stakeholders
- Facilitate training sessions and enablement activities for new processes or tools
- Coordinate with Project Manager and stakeholders to ensure a smooth transition

### Goals
- Maximize adoption of new processes, tools, and ways of working
- Reduce change-related friction and resistance across the organization
- Ensure all team members understand and feel supported through transitions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Bridges **Project Manager**, **Product Owner**, and all team members to maintain alignment on changes
- Works with **Project Manager** to align change management activities with project milestones
- Ensures process changes introduced by **Product Owner** and delivery teams are understood and adopted across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Change announcements and impact summaries distributed to all stakeholders
- Training schedules and enablement materials
- Feedback reports and adoption metrics shared with Project Manager and leadership
